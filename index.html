PAUSE IN THE JOURNEY

I was on the train, heading home after a long day, when I noticed a guy across from me slowly nodding off. His head dipped forward as he fought the pull of sleep, but eventually, he gave in. His eyes closed, and his body relaxed into the rhythm of the train. It was one of those everyday moments you almost miss if you’re not paying attention.

As I watched him sleep, I started thinking about how transit isn’t just about getting from point A to point B. It’s a space where life unfolds—where people find a moment to rest, to recharge, even if just for a few stops. The train, with its steady motion and soft hum, became a temporary refuge, a place where the city’s chaos quiets down just enough to allow a weary passenger to catch some rest.

It made me realize that transit is more than just a means of transportation—it’s a reflection of the city itself. It’s where we see the vulnerability of others, where we’re reminded of our shared humanity. Whether we’re rushing to work, heading home, or dozing off for a few precious minutes, the train is where we all come together. It’s a place where the city’s energy is channeled, where our paths cross in ways we don’t always expect.

Seeing that guy asleep on the train was a reminder that, even in the constant motion of city life, there are moments of stillness. In those moments, we find a connection to the transit that carries us through our day, and maybe even a connection to the people we share that journey with.
